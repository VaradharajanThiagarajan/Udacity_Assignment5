(2) What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform? Justify your choices.

Null Hypothesis : $H_{0}$¶
For the overall population, Average Time taken to read the congruent word ($\mu_{C}$) is equal to Average time taken to read the Incongruent Word ($\mu_{I}$)¶

$H_{0}: \mu_{C}$ = $\mu_{I}$

Alternate Hypothesis : $H_{1}$¶
For the overall population, Average Time taken to read the congruent word ($\mu_{C}$) is Not equal to Average time taken to read the Incongruent Word ($\mu_{I}$)¶

$H_{1}: \mu_{C}$ $\mu_{I}$

To infer the population mean based on the given sample data - we can use 'Z' Test or 'T' Test. Since we have sample size less than 30, then it and also we do not have the population standard deviation (which will make 'Z' test effective) - so 'T' Test will be more meaningful for our scenario. we will be using Paired 'T' Test since our given sample data has pair of observations (one for congruent and another for incongruent). It will be two tailed test since as per our alternate hypothesis we have the condition population average time for congruent word is not equal to population average time for Incongruent word .¶
we have assumptions as listed below¶

The observations are independent of one another.¶

The dependent variable should not contain any outliers.¶

The dependent variable should be approximately normally distributed.¶
